# Contributor License Agreement (CLA)

Before your contribution can be accepted, you must sign a
**Microsoft Contributor License Agreement (CLA)**.

## What is a CLA?

A Contributor License Agreement is a legal document that grants the project
maintainers the rights necessary to use, modify, and distribute your
contribution under the project's open-source license. It protects both you and
Microsoft — it does **not** transfer copyright ownership of your work; it simply
grants a license.

## How to sign

When you submit a pull request to this repository, the
**Microsoft CLA bot** will automatically post a comment indicating whether a
signed CLA is required. If it is:

1. Click the link provided by the bot in the PR comment.
2. Sign in with your GitHub account at **<https://cla.opensource.microsoft.com>**.
3. Review and accept the agreement.

Once signed, the bot marks your PR as CLA-compliant and future PRs from the
same GitHub account do not require re-signing.

> **Note:** You only need to sign the CLA once. It applies to all Microsoft
> open-source repositories.

## CLA vs. License

| | CLA | LICENSE |
|---|---|---|
| Who signs | Each contributor | N/A (governs users) |
| What it covers | Your right to contribute | Users' right to use the software |
| Where it lives | <https://cla.opensource.microsoft.com> | [LICENSE](LICENSE) file |
